At the end of February, we presented our new web sources capture and research desk at [Medieninnovationszentrum Babelsberg (MIZ)](https://miz-babelsberg.de), currently under development by [DOT Studio](https://dotstudio.eu) and myself as part of the [Digital Evidence Preservation Toolkit](https://www.digital-evidence.dev).

We outlined the need for investigative journalism newsrooms to defend against their online sources disappearing by creating their own record, and supporting their reporting by republishing verified "capsules" – as we used to do with Twitter embeds (RIP).

Thanks to the great team and camera folks at MIZ Babelsberg for hosting, and to the other speakers and experts: [Caroline Lindekamp](https://www.linkedin.com/in/caroline-lindekamp/), [Marion Franke](https://www.linkedin.com/in/marion-franke/) and [Volker Bach](https://www.linkedin.com/in/volker-bach/), [Claudia Hentze](https://www.linkedin.com/in/claudia-hentze/) from [PROGRESS Archive](https://progress.film/), [Katrin Gottschalk](https://www.linkedin.com/in/katrin-gottschalk/) from [taz](https://taz.de/), [Matthias Bannert](https://www.linkedin.com/in/matthias-bannert/), and [Olga Patlan](https://www.linkedin.com/in/olga-patlan/) for hosting and MC-ing.
